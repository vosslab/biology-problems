# List of Biochemistry Topics

Biochemistry is the study of chemical substances and vital processes in living organisms.
Biochemists focus on the role, function, and structure of biomolecules and the chemistry behind biological processes.
Questions ask students to identify molecular structures, classify biomolecules, calculate enzyme kinetics, and analyze chemical reactions like pH, pKa, and thermodynamics.

## Topics

1. [Life Molecules](topic01/index.md) &mdash; <a href="https://bio.libretexts.org/Courses/Roosevelt_University/BCHM_355_455_Biochemistry_(Roosevelt_University)/01%3A_Unit_1_-_Proteins/1.01%3A_Molecules_of_Life" target="_blank" rel="noopener" title="Open LibreTexts chapter"><span style="font-size: 0.8em;">(LibreTexts Unit 1, Chapter 1 <i class="fa fa-external-link-alt"></i>)</span></a>
    - Students categorize biomolecules into major classes (proteins, carbohydrates, lipids, nucleic acids) and distinguish hydrophobic from hydrophilic molecules.

2. [Water and pH](topic02/index.md) &mdash; <a href="https://bio.libretexts.org/Courses/Roosevelt_University/BCHM_355_455_Biochemistry_(Roosevelt_University)/01%3A_Unit_1_-_Proteins/1.02%3A_Chemistry_of_Water" target="_blank" rel="noopener" title="Open LibreTexts chapter"><span style="font-size: 0.8em;">(LibreTexts Unit 1, Chapter 2 <i class="fa fa-external-link-alt"></i>)</span></a>
    - Students calculate pH from pKa using the Henderson-Hasselbalch equation, predict protonation states of simple acids and bases, and analyze buffer systems.

3. [Amino Acids](topic03/index.md) &mdash; <a href="https://bio.libretexts.org/Courses/Roosevelt_University/BCHM_355_455_Biochemistry_(Roosevelt_University)/01%3A_Unit_1_-_Proteins/1.03%3A_Amino_Acids_and_Peptides" target="_blank" rel="noopener" title="Open LibreTexts chapter"><span style="font-size: 0.8em;">(LibreTexts Unit 1, Chapter 3 <i class="fa fa-external-link-alt"></i>)</span></a>

4. [Protein Structure](topic04/index.md) &mdash; <a href="https://bio.libretexts.org/Courses/Roosevelt_University/BCHM_355_455_Biochemistry_(Roosevelt_University)/01%3A_Unit_1_-_Proteins/1.04%3A_Proteins-_Structure_and_Folding" target="_blank" rel="noopener" title="Open LibreTexts chapter"><span style="font-size: 0.8em;">(LibreTexts Unit 1, Chapter 4 <i class="fa fa-external-link-alt"></i>)</span></a>
    - Students identify levels of protein structure, distinguish alpha helices from beta sheets, analyze backbone hydrogen bonding patterns, and interpret Ramachandran plots.

5. [Protein Purification](topic05/index.md) &mdash; <a href="https://bio.libretexts.org/Courses/Roosevelt_University/BCHM_355_455_Biochemistry_(Roosevelt_University)/01%3A_Unit_1_-_Proteins/1.05%3A_Protein_Purification" target="_blank" rel="noopener" title="Open LibreTexts chapter"><span style="font-size: 0.8em;">(LibreTexts Unit 1, Chapter 5 <i class="fa fa-external-link-alt"></i>)</span></a>
    - Students choose purification methods for proteins based on size, charge, and binding affinity, and interpret results from gel filtration, ion exchange, SDS-PAGE, and isoelectric focusing.

6. [Thermodynamics](topic06/index.md) &mdash; <a href="https://bio.libretexts.org/Courses/Roosevelt_University/BCHM_355_455_Biochemistry_(Roosevelt_University)/02%3A_Unit_2_-_Enzymes/2.01%3A_Enzyme_Thermodynamics" target="_blank" rel="noopener" title="Open LibreTexts chapter"><span style="font-size: 0.8em;">(LibreTexts Unit 2, Chapter 1 <i class="fa fa-external-link-alt"></i>)</span></a>

7. [Enzyme Kinetics](topic07/index.md) &mdash; <a href="https://bio.libretexts.org/Courses/Roosevelt_University/BCHM_355_455_Biochemistry_(Roosevelt_University)/02%3A_Unit_2_-_Enzymes/2.02%3A_Enzyme_Kinetics" target="_blank" rel="noopener" title="Open LibreTexts chapter"><span style="font-size: 0.8em;">(LibreTexts Unit 2, Chapter 2 <i class="fa fa-external-link-alt"></i>)</span></a>
    - Students determine Vmax and Km from substrate concentration data, interpret Michaelis-Menten and Lineweaver-Burk plots, and calculate enzyme efficiency using kcat.

8. [Enzyme Inhibition](topic08/index.md) &mdash; <a href="https://bio.libretexts.org/Courses/Roosevelt_University/BCHM_355_455_Biochemistry_(Roosevelt_University)/02%3A_Unit_2_-_Enzymes/2.03%3A_Enzyme_Inhibition" target="_blank" rel="noopener" title="Open LibreTexts chapter"><span style="font-size: 0.8em;">(LibreTexts Unit 2, Chapter 3 <i class="fa fa-external-link-alt"></i>)</span></a>
    - Students classify inhibition type (competitive, noncompetitive, uncompetitive, mixed) from changes in Km and Vmax in Michaelis-Menten data, and interpret inhibitor effects on Lineweaver-Burk plots.

9. [Enzyme Allostery](topic09/index.md) &mdash; <a href="https://bio.libretexts.org/Courses/Roosevelt_University/BCHM_355_455_Biochemistry_(Roosevelt_University)/02%3A_Unit_2_-_Enzymes/2.04%3A_Enzyme_Allostery" target="_blank" rel="noopener" title="Open LibreTexts chapter"><span style="font-size: 0.8em;">(LibreTexts Unit 2, Chapter 4 <i class="fa fa-external-link-alt"></i>)</span></a>
    - Students analyze allosteric regulation, cooperativity, and sigmoidal binding curves, calculate Hill coefficients from hemoglobin oxygen binding data, and trace end-product feedback control through metabolic pathway diagrams.

10. [Carbohydrates](topic10/index.md) &mdash; <a href="https://bio.libretexts.org/Courses/Roosevelt_University/BCHM_355_455_Biochemistry_(Roosevelt_University)/03%3A_Unit_3_-_Macromolecules/3.01%3A_Carbohydrates" target="_blank" rel="noopener" title="Open LibreTexts chapter"><span style="font-size: 0.8em;">(LibreTexts Unit 3, Chapter 1 <i class="fa fa-external-link-alt"></i>)</span></a>
    - Students classify monosaccharides using Fischer and Haworth projections, identify anomers and epimers, and analyze glycosidic bond formation in disaccharides.

11. [Nucleic Acids](topic11/index.md) &mdash; <a href="https://bio.libretexts.org/Courses/Roosevelt_University/BCHM_355_455_Biochemistry_(Roosevelt_University)/03%3A_Unit_3_-_Macromolecules/3.02%3A_Nucleic_Acids" target="_blank" rel="noopener" title="Open LibreTexts chapter"><span style="font-size: 0.8em;">(LibreTexts Unit 3, Chapter 2 <i class="fa fa-external-link-alt"></i>)</span></a>
    - Students identify nucleotide components, compare DNA and RNA chemical structure, name nucleosides and nucleotides, and analyze phosphodiester bond formation.

12. [Lipids](topic12/index.md) &mdash; <a href="https://bio.libretexts.org/Courses/Roosevelt_University/BCHM_355_455_Biochemistry_(Roosevelt_University)/03%3A_Unit_3_-_Macromolecules/3.03%3A_Lipids" target="_blank" rel="noopener" title="Open LibreTexts chapter"><span style="font-size: 0.8em;">(LibreTexts Unit 3, Chapter 3 <i class="fa fa-external-link-alt"></i>)</span></a>
    - Students classify lipids by type (fatty acids, triacylglycerols, sphingolipids, steroids, waxes), identify lipid structures from molecular formulas, and distinguish saturated from unsaturated fatty acids.

13. [Membranes and Membrane Proteins](topic13/index.md) &mdash; <a href="https://bio.libretexts.org/Courses/Roosevelt_University/BCHM_355_455_Biochemistry_(Roosevelt_University)/03%3A_Unit_3_-_Macromolecules/3.04%3A_Membranes_and_Membrane_Proteins" target="_blank" rel="noopener" title="Open LibreTexts chapter"><span style="font-size: 0.8em;">(LibreTexts Unit 3, Chapter 4 <i class="fa fa-external-link-alt"></i>)</span></a>
    - Students analyze phospholipid bilayer structure, predict how composition affects membrane fluidity, classify integral and peripheral membrane proteins, and explain transport mechanisms.

14. [Human Senses](topic14/index.md) &mdash; <a href="https://bio.libretexts.org/Courses/Roosevelt_University/BCHM_355_455_Biochemistry_(Roosevelt_University)/04%3A_Biochemistry_of_Our_Senses" target="_blank" rel="noopener" title="Open LibreTexts chapter"><span style="font-size: 0.8em;">(LibreTexts Unit 4, Chapter 1 <i class="fa fa-external-link-alt"></i>)</span></a>
    - Students explain the biochemical basis of taste, smell, hearing, sight, and touch perception.

15. [Digestion](topic15/index.md)
    - Students trace the digestion of proteins, carbohydrates, and lipids into smaller units through enzyme action, explain ATP hydrolysis and energy coupling, identify activated carriers and vitamin-derived cofactors, and describe how metabolic pathways are regulated through enzyme levels, catalytic activity, and substrate availability.

16. [Sugar Metabolism](topic16/index.md)
    - Students trace glucose through glycolysis to pyruvate, calculate net ATP and NADH yield, explain how fructose and galactose enter the pathway, describe NAD+ regeneration through fermentation, identify regulatory control points responsive to energy demand and hormonal signals, and contrast gluconeogenesis bypass reactions with irreversible glycolytic steps including reciprocal regulation between the two pathways.

17. [Oxidative Phosphorylation](topic17/index.md)
    - Students describe pyruvate conversion to acetyl-CoA by the pyruvate dehydrogenase complex, trace acetyl-CoA oxidation through the citric acid cycle tracking NADH and FADH2 production, explain how the electron transport chain creates a proton gradient through sequential redox reactions with oxygen as final acceptor, describe ATP synthase as a rotary enzyme coupling proton flow to ATP formation, and calculate the overall yield of approximately 30 ATP per glucose.

18. [Lipid Metabolism](topic18/index.md)
    - Students trace fatty acid mobilization and beta-oxidation in mitochondria generating acetyl-CoA, NADH, and FADH2, explain ketone body formation during fasting, describe cytoplasmic fatty acid synthesis from malonyl-CoA through palmitate using NADPH, outline cholesterol synthesis and its role as precursor for steroid hormones and bile salts, and explain lipoprotein transport of lipids in the bloodstream including roles in cholesterol distribution and disease risk.

19. [Glycogen and Pentose Phosphate](topic19/index.md)
    - Students describe glycogen degradation by phosphorylase and debranching enzymes, explain reciprocal regulation of glycogen synthesis and breakdown through insulin, glucagon, epinephrine, and phosphorylation cascades, trace glucose 6-phosphate through the pentose phosphate pathway generating NADPH for reductive biosynthesis and ribose 5-phosphate for nucleotide synthesis, and explain how metabolic flux adjusts based on cellular needs for reducing power, precursors, or energy.

20. [Photosynthesis](topic20/index.md)
    - Students trace light energy capture through Photosystem II and Photosystem I, explain water oxidation and oxygen release, describe electron transport and proton gradient formation driving ATP synthase, trace NADPH and ATP into the Calvin cycle where Rubisco fixes CO2 into hexose phosphates, and compare C4 and CAM adaptations for improved carbon fixation efficiency.

21. [Nitrogen Metabolism](topic21/index.md)
    - Students trace amino acid degradation through transamination to glutamate and ammonium entry into the urea cycle, classify carbon skeletons entering glycolysis or citric acid cycle, describe amino acid synthesis through glutamate and glutamine intermediates, outline pyrimidine and purine nucleotide biosynthesis pathways, explain deoxyribonucleotide formation and nucleotide salvage, and identify regulatory mechanisms including feedback inhibition.
