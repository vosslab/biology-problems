# List of Molecular Biology Topics

Molecular biology is the study of the central dogma: replication, transcription, translation, and cell function at the molecular level.
Molecular biologists study how genetic information is copied, read, and expressed as proteins.
Questions ask students to transcribe DNA to mRNA, translate codons to amino acids, identify consensus sequences, analyze reading frames, and understand gene regulation through operons and promoters.

## Topics

1. [Introduction to Molecular Biology](topic01/index.md)
    - What is molecular biology, cells, organisms, gene creatures, genes, and chromosomes.

2. [Meiosis and DNA Structure](topic02/index.md)
    - Meiosis, DNA composition, base pairing, and double-helix structure.

3. [Protein Structure and Chromosomes](topic03/index.md)
    - Protein structure, the central dogma, and chromosome organization.

4. [Manipulation of Nucleic Acids](topic04/index.md)
    - Restriction enzymes, gel electrophoresis, and PCR.

5. [Cloning and Sequencing](topic05/index.md)
    - Gene cloning, DNA sequencing methods, and RT-qPCR.

6. [DNA Replication](topic06/index.md)
    - Replication machinery, origins, forks, and fidelity mechanisms.

7. [Transcription](topic07/index.md)
    - Transcription of genes, RNA polymerase, promoters, and regulation.

8. [RNA Processing and CRISPR](topic08/index.md)
    - RNA splicing, mRNA maturation, and CRISPR gene editing.

9. [Translation](topic09/index.md)
    - Translation of mRNA into protein, codons, anticodons, genetic code, ribosomes, tRNA, and peptide synthesis.

10. [Historical Experiments](topic10/index.md)
    - Landmark experiments that established core principles of molecular biology: Griffith's transformation experiment, Avery-MacLeod-McCarty's identification of DNA as the transforming principle, Hershey-Chase's confirmation that DNA carries genetic information, and Beadle-Tatum's one gene-one enzyme hypothesis linking genes to proteins.
